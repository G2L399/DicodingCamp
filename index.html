Major Space Events

Explore some of the most awe-inspiring space phenomena observed throughout history.

Supernova SN 1604 (Kepler's Supernova)

SN 1604, also known as Kepler's Supernova, Kepler's Nova or Kepler's Star, was a Type Ia supernova that occurred in the Milky Way, in the constellation Ophiuchus. Appearing in 1604, it is the most recent supernova in the Milky Way galaxy to have been unquestionably observed by the naked eye, occurring no farther than 6 kiloparsecs (20,000 light-years) from Earth. Before the adoption of the current naming system for supernovae, it was named for Johannes Kepler, the German astronomer who described it in De Stella Nova.

Great Comet of 1680

This comet, known for its strikingly bright tail, was visible in December 1680. It captivated observers and was among the first comets to be studied with a telescope.

Great Meteor Storm of 1833

The Leonid Meteor Shower of November 1833 created a spectacle as thousands of meteors lit up the sky every hour. This event spurred advancements in meteor science.

Great Comet of 1843

In March 1843, a comet with an exceptionally long tail, measuring over 2 astronomical units, became a breathtaking sight, especially in the southern hemisphere.

Great Conjunction of 2020

On December 21, 2020, Jupiter and Saturn appeared a mere 0.1 degrees apart. This rare alignment, the closest since 1623, was popularly called the "Christmas Star."
